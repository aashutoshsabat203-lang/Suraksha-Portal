Essential Safety Guidelines

Situational Awareness

Maintain constant awareness of your surroundings, trust your instincts, and avoid isolated areas after dark. Use location-sharing apps with trusted contacts for enhanced safety.

Self-Defense Strategies

Learn practical techniques through accredited courses. Carry approved safety devices like pepper spray, whistles, or personal alarms legally permitted in your state.

Safe Travel Protocols

Prioritize women-only transportation options. Use verified ride-sharing services, keep emergency contacts handy, and check destination reviews before travel.

Legal Protection Framework

Access comprehensive guides on Indian laws including POCSO Act, Domestic Violence Act, Cybercrime provisions, and legal aid services.

Learn About Laws

Here you can find information about key Indian laws related to women's safety:

  • Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012: Protects children from sexual assault, sexual harassment, and pornography.
  • Domestic Violence Act, 2005: Provides protection to women from domestic violence of all forms (physical, emotional, sexual, economic).
  • Indian Penal Code (IPC) Sections: Various sections address crimes against women, including rape (376), assault to outrage modesty (354), kidnapping (363), dowry death (304B), etc.
  • Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000 (Cybercrime Provisions): Addresses cyberstalking, online harassment, and other digital crimes against women.
  • Legal Aid Services: Information on how to access free legal aid provided by the government through Legal Services Authorities.

For detailed legal advice, please consult a legal professional or contact the National Legal Services Authority (NALSA).
